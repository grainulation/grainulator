How it works

It researches like you would — if you had 4 hours and zero ego.

Grainulator gathers findings, grades each one by confidence level, and then challenges its own assumptions. That 6‑week migration estimate? It is actually 12. You would rather know now.

Grainulator demo: The tool initializes a sprint asking "Should we migrate to Postgres?" for engineering leadership. It researches postgres migration feasibility and gathers 6 findings: Postgres handles 12k TPS (factual), migration estimated at 6 weeks (estimate), pgvector eliminates separate vector DB (factual), no team Postgres experience (risk), current DB license costs $48k per year (factual), and recommends a 90-day parallel-run. The tool then challenges its own estimate, finding the 6-week timeline contradicts the team experience gap and industry benchmarks of 11.4 weeks, revising to 12 weeks. Finally it compiles a brief with coverage scores: query performance 92%, migration 87%, operations risk 94%, team skill 71%. Result: PASS, brief ready.
